Projects

Borderland Statecraft: Armed Politics in Southeast Asia’s Contested Borderlands

Book project, under contract with Cornell University Press

Growing out of my PhD at SOAS University of London, this book project examines how armed organisations such as the Wa and Kokang have navigated between the Myanmar state and China. It argues that China has shaped their trajectories not only as a neighbouring power, but also as a political model, an institutional influence, and a historical force.

By tracing how non-state armed actors selectively adapted forms of party organisation, territorial administration, and governance, the project explains why political authority in the China–Myanmar borderlands has developed along divergent paths.

Borderland Capitalism: the Chinese-run online scam industry in Southeast Asia

Three-year project, Hallsworth Fellowship, University of Manchester, 2026–2029

This project examines the online scam industry and the wider political economy of digital illicit economies across Southeast Asia. It explores how scam operations are embedded in borderland governance, logistical systems, labour regimes, and cross-border infrastructures, and how they endure through adaptation, mobility, and regional reorganisation.

Logistics Politics in the Cracks: Digital Illicit Economies at the Margins

Second book project

This second book project examines the political economy of digital scam industries, illicit logistics, and centre–periphery relations across Southeast Asia and West Africa. It develops a broader argument about how digital illicit economies remain embedded in wider political and economic structures rather than existing outside them.

Female Scholars in Conflict Studies

Convened workshop series, SOAS University of London, 2025–2026

This research-led workshop series focuses on methods, ethics, gender, and access in the study of conflict and hostile contexts. It provides a platform for methodological reflection and intellectual exchange among scholars working on politically sensitive and difficult field sites.

Selected workshops:
“Researching the Digital Illicit Economy: Methods, Ethics, and Access” (March 2026).
“Doing Ethnography in Hostile Contexts: A Conversation with Professor Ching Kwan Lee” (February 2026).
“Female Scholars in Conflict Studies: Research Methods Reflections” (July 2025).

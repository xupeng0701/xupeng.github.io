About

Dr Xu Peng is Hallsworth Research Fellow in Political Economy at the University of Manchester.

Her research examines conflict, illicit economies, borderlands, and China–Southeast Asia relations, with a particular focus on Myanmar and the China–Myanmar borderland.

Trained in political science, she adopts a historically grounded approach that integrates archival research, fieldwork-based oral histories, and political economy analysis.

Publications

  • Peng, Xu. “The Enduring Legacy and Historical Continuity of Kokang's Mutinies in the China-Myanmar Borderlands.” Journal of Contemporary Asia (2025).
  • Peng, Xu. “Playing with Fire: How Engagement with Illicit Economies Shapes the Survival and Resilience of Ethnic Armed Organisations in the China-Myanmar Borderlands.” China Perspectives (2024).

Projects

Borderland Capitalism: the Chinese-run online scam industry in Southeast Asia
A three-year project examining digital illicit economies, conflict-affected borderlands, and cross-border political economy in Southeast Asia.

Borderland Statecraft: Armed Politics in Southeast Asia’s Contested Borderlands
A book project based on comparative research on the Wa and Kokang in the China–Myanmar borderland.

Logistics Politics in the Cracks: Digital Illicit Economies at the Margins
A second book project on the political economy of digital scam industries, illicit logistics, and centre–periphery relations.

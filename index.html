About

Dr Xu Peng is Hallsworth Research Fellow in Political Economy at the University of Manchester. Her research examines conflict, illicit economies, borderlands, and China–Southeast Asia relations, with a particular focus on Myanmar and the China–Myanmar borderland.

Trained in political science, she adopts a historically grounded approach that integrates archival research, fieldwork-based oral histories, and political economy analysis. Her work speaks to wider questions in International Relations and Political Economy concerning sovereignty, order, conflict, and power.

Projects

My research is organised around two major book projects on armed politics, borderland governance, and digital illicit economies, alongside a research-led workshop series on methods, gender, and fieldwork in conflict settings.

Borderland Statecraft: Armed Politics in Southeast Asia’s Contested Borderlands

Book project, under contract with Cornell University Press

Growing out of my PhD at SOAS University of London (2020–2024), this book project examines how armed organisations such as the Wa and Kokang have navigated between the Myanmar state and China. It argues that China has shaped their trajectories not only as a neighbouring power, but also as a political model, an institutional influence, and a historical force.

By tracing how non-state armed actors selectively adapted Chinese forms of party organisation, territorial administration, and governance, the project explains why political authority in the China–Myanmar borderlands has developed along divergent paths.

Logistics Politics in the Cracks: Digital Illicit Economies at the Margins

Second book project

My second book project examines the online scam industry and the wider political economy of digital illicit economies across Southeast Asia and beyond. It explores how scam operations are embedded in borderland governance, logistical systems, and transnational infrastructures, and how they reshape relations between sovereignty, mobility, labour, and violence.

More broadly, the project uses empirically grounded regional research to address wider International Relations questions about sovereignty, order, conflict, and power.

Female Scholars in Conflict Studies

Convened workshop series, SOAS University of London, 2025–2026

I convene the Female Scholars in Conflict Studies workshop series, a research-led initiative that brings together discussions on methods, ethics, gender, and access in the study of conflict and hostile contexts. The series provides a platform for methodological reflection, intellectual exchange, and support for scholars working on difficult field sites and politically sensitive topics.
